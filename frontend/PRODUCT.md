# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Sophisticated and institutional investors (HNWIs, family offices, LP networks) evaluating premium coastal real estate exposure in Vietnam, specifically Quy Nhon. They arrive doing due diligence: they want evidence, track record, and research depth before they trust capital to a fund. Context is considered, desk-bound, comparison-driven — not impulse.

## Product Purpose
EV Investment is a marketing/landing site that bridges premium coastal real estate development in Quy Nhon with institutional-grade investors. It presents the investment thesis (why Quy Nhon), the asset portfolio, an interactive ROI calculator, the research department's macro reports, and the team. Success = the visitor reads EV as a credible, institutional counterparty and requests an advisory session.

## Brand Personality
Institutional, composed, trustworthy. Three words: rigorous, restrained, credible. The voice is that of a serious investment house (Bridgewater/Stronghold-adjacent), not a design studio or a hype-driven crypto fund. Confidence is shown through specificity (numbers, research, methodology), never through spectacle.

## Anti-references
- Web3/crypto-fund maximalism: neon gradients, glassmorphism, animated everything.
- Generic SaaS template: identical card grids, hero-metric template, tracked-out eyebrow over every section.
- Consumer real-estate-listing portals (Zillow-style): busy, transactional, downmarket.
- "Design studio" expressiveness that undercuts trust. Motion must be quiet and earned.

## Design Principles
1. **Earn trust through evidence.** Specific numbers, named research, real methodology over adjectives.
2. **Restraint as a signal of seriousness.** Quiet motion, disciplined palette, no spectacle — composure reads as institutional.
3. **Hierarchy carries the message.** Playfair display for thesis-level statements, Inter for body and tracked labels; let scale and weight do the work.
4. **One accent, used sparingly.** Teal leads; gold is a rare punctuation, never wallpaper.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` (all entrance/scroll motion collapses to fade/instant). Maintain WCAG AA contrast for body and large text on the dark navy base. Light-on-dark body copy gets slightly looser line-height for legibility.
